History

Founding (950 ASE)

The land of Varlesia has changed hands and had boundaries moved countless times throughout history. Rulership has been passed between elves, and dwarves, and orcs - and even previously humans, but Varlesia as we know it was only founded after the land was wrest from the grip of the Adal Elves.

Under Queen Adalia (950 - 987 ASE)

During her time as Queen of Varlesia, Adalia was known as "Adalia the Seer". It was said that she held many mystic conclaves with wise men and woman across the realms, who she sought to aid her govern this new land.

Under King Graveth (1023-1023 ASE)

King Graveth held the throne of Varlesia for a total of 12 days. His reign was short, but was accepted by the people, though his coronation was a rushed affair. An event that often lasts days spanned only 38 minutes. It is thought that his advisors were aware of the threat that his elder, bastard brother Amavet posed to his crown, and so they attempted to put Graveth in power, then hide him away. As you will no-doubt tell by now, they failed.

Graveth the Unfortunate, as he is now known, was rushed from Capital and taken to as discrete monastery, just on the edge of the river Felder, at the foot of the Myriad Mountains. He went disguised and accompanied by only a handful of guards. All those were clad in plain clothes, without sigil or sign to show their affiliation. They had guessed that they would avoid Amavet with this element of subterfuge - and they were correct! What they didn't count on was the criminal element.

While King Graveth technically reigned for 12 days, he spent 9 of those days in the hands of his captors - a band of villains known as the Copper Company. They struck the monastery in what appeared to be a raid unrelated to the King's presence, but they took his guards by surprise. It is told that the King's guards fought valiantly, but were eventually overwhelmed, and slain. The King, being a mere 12 years of age, put up much less of a fight. However, the Company seems to fracture at this point - half of the remaining number taking off back into the Beowode, while the others remained in the monastery. This schism still remains a mystery, though what is clear, is that the activity at the monastery drew the attention of Amavet quite quickly.

Some have speculated that Amavet had connections to the Copper Company, which led him to so quickly find the hiding place of the King, and resulted in such lack of bloodshed on this occasion - as when he arrived, Amavet found his brother chained to the central pillar of the monastery. Not one for pleasantries, Amavet slew him where he lay and collected "what he was due" - the crown of Varlesia.

The site where King Graveth met his end is now where the church in the town of Gravesford was established - the name of the town a reference to this ill-fated monarch.

Under Saint Manfred (1053 ASE - Current)

Those present on the day Manfred slew Omund have remained tight-lipped on many details, but there are some facts that have filtered through the populace - though Manfred and his allies would surely claim these as falsehoods. It is said he laughed. Manfred laughed as his crushed the former King's windpipe with his gauntleted fist. He laughed as he sank his blade through the former King's heart. He laughed, as he cast Omund's broken corpse down at the feet of his men, who wept at the sight of their destroyed monarch.
